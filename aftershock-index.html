Aftershock Index · Inaugural Edition · October 2026

The Advocacy Gap: measured for the first time.

The Aftershock Index is the annual research report on self-advocacy among Black and brown women in corporate America. Drawn from a community survey of 1,247 respondents, an employer sentiment audit of 184 HR leaders, and longitudinal cohort data from the Rise Ready Academy.

Executive Summary

Five findings that change the conversation.

Each finding below is shareable as a standalone graphic. The full 28-page report includes industry-by-industry breakdowns, employer sentiment data, and the Aftershock 25 — community members whose stories exemplify the data.

Finding 01
78%

experienced appearance-based discrimination at work in the last 12 months

Hair texture, clothing style, and body shape were the three most-reported triggers — with hair texture cited by 64% of respondents alone.

Finding 02
23%

formally advocated for themselves in response

The remaining 77% reported that the cost of speaking up — to career trajectory, peer relationships, or psychological safety — was higher than the cost of staying silent.

Finding 03
55pt

The Advocacy Gap™ — Rise Ready's signature metric

The 55-point gap between discrimination experienced and self-advocacy taken is the most predictive variable in the dataset. It outweighs industry, age, and seniority combined.

Finding 04
+41%

confidence lift after a structured Rise Ready cohort

Before/after measurement of cohort participants on a 10-point self-advocacy scale shows that targeted training closes the gap meaningfully — and rapidly.

Finding 05
8.2×

employer perception vs. employee experience

HR leaders are 8.2 times more likely to describe their workplace as "equitable for women of color" than the women of color who actually work there. This is the Corporate Blind Spot.

Finding 06
$1.8M

in documented compensation gains across the Rise Ready community

Total raises, bonuses, and promotions secured by community members in the last 12 months — measured at the Win Wall, verified at the Impact Ledger.
